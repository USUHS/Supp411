Does FDA approve supplements?
The Food and Drug Administration (FDA) does not even see, look at, or know about the existence of a dietary supplement product until a problem is brought to their attention.
 
FDA regulates dietary supplements but they do not evaluate or approve them before they are sold. 

Just because a dietary supplement is on the shelf (being sold) does not mean that it is safe or effective. 

If you see an "FDA Approved" seal on a dietary supplement bottle or label, that is a false claim. All dietary supplements should include the FDA disclaimer stating "These statements have not been evaluated by the Food and Drug Administration."
